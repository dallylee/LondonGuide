  • 4. The Famous Blue Door 📍 (280 Westbourne Park Road)

    15-min walk north from Portobello. THE iconic blue door to Will's flat! Note: This is a private residence - be respectful! Quick photo from the street, no knocking. The door was auctioned off in 2020 but the location remains a pilgrimage site.

    5-min walk from the blue door. The private communal garden where Will climbed over the fence! You can view the lovely gardens through the gates (private access for residents only).

  • 6. The Ritz Hotel 📍

    Back in central London (near your hotel!). Where Anna stayed. Gorgeous exterior - walk past on your way back or visit another day. Free to admire from outside; afternoon tea available if you want to splurge (£70+, book ahead).

🛡️ Safety & Street-Smarts

London is generally very safe, but stay alert! Here are the key precautions:

📱 Phone Theft Prevention

  • Stand away from the curb when using your phone - thieves on bikes/mopeds snatch phones
  • Back against building when taking photos at Westminster, Tower Bridge, busy streets
  • Hold phone with firm grip - don't dangle it loosely
  • At cafes, never leave phone on outer tables where someone could grab it
  • In evenings, check directions under shop awnings, then tuck phone away

👜 Bag & Belongings Security

  • Use cross-body bag with zipper, keep it in front in crowds
  • Never put wallet/phone in back pocket
  • On Tube/buses: hold bag on lap or tight by side
  • In restaurants: don't hang bag on chair back - keep strap around leg
  • Pickpockets work in teams - one distracts, one steals. Stay alert!
  • High-risk areas: Oxford Street, Covent Garden, Leicester Square, Borough Market

🚨 Emergency Numbers

999 - Police, Ambulance, Fire (immediate emergency)

101 - Police non-emergency (report theft after the fact)

112 - Works same as 999 (EU standard emergency number)

111 - NHS medical advice (non-emergency health questions)

Text 61016 - British Transport Police (if unsafe on public transport)

⚠️ Common Scams to Avoid

  • Charity Petition Scam:

    People with clipboards ask you to sign "petition," then aggressively demand donation. While distracted, accomplice may pickpocket. Solution: Don't stop! Say "No, thank you" and keep walking.

  • Friendship Bracelet Scam:

    Someone ties string bracelet on your wrist then demands £10-20. Don't let anyone grab your hand! If they tie something, refuse to pay, remove it, walk away.

  • Shell Game / Cups & Balls:

    Seen on Westminster Bridge. You cannot win - it's rigged! While you watch, pickpockets work the crowd. Don't stop to watch street gambling.

  • Photo Characters:

    People in costumes (Mickey Mouse, etc.) offer photos then demand £5-10 tip. Only pose if you're willing to pay!

🌙 Night Safety

  • Soho/Piccadilly are busy and safe till 11pm - theatre crowds, diners
  • Stick to main, well-lit streets - Shaftesbury Ave, Regent St, Leicester Sq
  • Avoid quiet alleyways, especially Walker's Court area
  • Ignore club promoters offering "free drinks" - skip with minor in group
  • Stay together at all times after dark
  • If anyone harasses you, walk into nearest shop/restaurant for help

👮 Trust Your Gut: If something feels off, remove yourselves. London has CCTV everywhere and police patrol tourist areas. You can approach any shop staff or police officer for help. You'll likely have zero incidents - just stay aware!

🚇 Transport Guide

London transport is easy once you know the basics! Use contactless cards and you're set.

💳 Contactless Payment & Caps

  • Daily Cap: £8.90 for zones 1-2 (unlimited Tube & bus rides!)
  • Bus-Only Cap: £5.25 per day
  • Single Bus Fare: £1.75 (tap in only, no tap out)
  • Hopper Fare: Unlimited buses within 1 hour = £1.75 total
  • Use ONE card per person - tap in/out on Tube, tap in only on buses
  • No cash accepted on buses - must use contactless card/phone

🚇 Tube (Underground) Basics

  • Always tap in AND out at yellow readers (forget = max fare charged)
  • Follow color-coded signs for your line (Piccadilly = dark blue, Bakerloo = brown)
  • Direction shown by terminus station (e.g. "towards Heathrow")
  • Mind the gap! Some platforms have large gaps between train and platform
  • Let passengers off first before boarding
  • Stand right on escalators, walk left - strict London rule!
  • Peak hours (Mon-Fri 6:30-9:30am & 4-7pm) get very crowded
  • Last trains around midnight; Night Tube runs Fri/Sat on some lines

🚌 Bus Tips

  • Wave/flag your bus as it approaches the stop
  • Enter front door, tap card at yellow reader
  • Press red "Stop" button one block before your destination
  • Exit via middle door (don't tap out!)
  • Sit upstairs front for best sightseeing views
  • Buses announce stops - listen for your destination
